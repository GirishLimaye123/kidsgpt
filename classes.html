MODULE 1

Builder Foundations

W1 Hello, AI + Your First Game

🕹️ Warm-up (before class)

Real or AI? — guess which photos, poems, and answers came from a machine.

💡 Big idea

AI is a prediction machine, not a person. It's amazing at patterns, and it can be confidently wrong — which is why the builder is always the boss.

✏️ Design corner

Feedback: how does a player know something happened? (Design Detective rounds 1–2.)

🔨 Build sprint

Prompt AI to build a 5-question quiz game on a topic you love. Then make AI change two things.

🛡️ Trust check

Play your own game. Is every answer key actually correct? Fix one AI mistake.

DAILY WIN A working quiz game published to the gallery. Open the full class page →

W2 The Quest Game

🕹️ Warm-up

State Spy: play a tiny game twice and list everything it "remembered" (hearts, coins, position).

💡 Big idea

Game state — a good game remembers what happened. Numbers that change are the heartbeat of every app.

✏️ Design corner

Fairness tuning: paper-test how many hearts and what coin prices feel fun vs. frustrating.

🔨 Build sprint

Quest prompt builder: 5 scenes, 10 challenges, lives, coins, power-ups — PLUS one unlocked twist you choose (timer, streak bonus, trap question, or boss with 2 hearts).

🛡️ Trust check

Paper prototype with a partner first. Watch silently. What confused them?

DAILY WIN A quest game with YOUR twist that nobody else has.

W3 The Story of You

🕹️ Warm-up

Story Detective Academy: choose the better interview question and unlock the rules for good listening.

💡 Big idea

AI can polish words, but humans make people feel understood. Ask permission before sharing real-person stories.

✏️ Design corner

Facts are ingredients. Story is the recipe. Listen for the feeling inside a fact.

🔨 Build sprint

Interview a partner, turn notes into a 6-line poem, design the deck, then ask AI to create a 6-slide HTML story.

🛡️ Trust check

Before upload, ask the partner: Is it true? Is it kind? Is anything too private? What should I change?

DAILY WIN A 6-slide mini documentary poem deck about a classmate. Open the full class page →

W4 Built for a Human

🕹️ Warm-up

Guess My Problem: read three real interview quotes, guess what the person actually needs.

💡 Big idea

Empathy first. Great builders solve someone's real problem — and the only way to find it is to ask and listen.

✏️ Design corner

Interview skills: open questions ("tell me about…") beat yes/no questions. Practice in pairs.

🔨 Build sprint

Interview a classmate about something annoying in their week. Build them a tiny tool: a homework randomizer, chore picker, practice timer — their problem decides.

🛡️ Trust check

Hand it to them. Watch them use it without helping. One fix based on what you saw.

DAILY WIN Someone in the room actually uses a thing you made for them.

W5 Memory Week

🕹️ Warm-up

The Goldfish Game: a game that forgets everything when you blink (reload). Feel the problem before learning the fix.

💡 Big idea

Persistence: apps can save. One prompt — "save my coins, hearts, and scene in localStorage" — and your game remembers you tomorrow.

✏️ Design corner

What's worth saving? High scores yes… but should a game save your name? Your photo? Where you live? (Privacy IS a design decision.)

🔨 Build sprint

Add saving + a high-score screen to your quest game. Bonus: a "new game" button that wipes the slate.

🛡️ Trust check

Close the browser. Reopen. Did it remember? Now find where the data lives (DevTools field trip).

DAILY WIN Your game greets you by your builder name and keeps your high score.

W6 AI Senses

🕹️ Warm-up

Sort It! — sort 12 things into buckets, then meet the plot twist: you just did what a classifier does.

💡 Big idea

Classification: AI learns categories from examples. More examples = smarter. Weird examples = weird AI. (Hello, bias.)

✏️ Design corner

Choosing training examples IS design. What happens if every "cat" photo you train on is orange?

🔨 Build sprint

Train a real image classifier (Teachable Machine) on hand signs or drawings, then wire it into a mini-game: rock-paper-scissors vs. the webcam.

🛡️ Trust check

Try to fool your own model. Where does it break? Why?

DAILY WIN An AI YOU trained recognizes your hand signs live.

W7 Art Direction

🕹️ Warm-up

Style Match: same character, six art styles — match each image to the prompt that made it.

💡 Big idea

AI image generation rewards intent: style words, character sheets, and consistency rules separate "random pictures" from "a world."

✏️ Design corner

Build a one-page style guide: 3 colors, 1 art style, 1 mood word. Every image must obey it.

🔨 Build sprint

Generate a hero sheet + five scene backgrounds for your quest game that all match your style guide. Swap them in.

🛡️ Trust check

Whose style is it? Talk about copying artists, logos, and why we make original worlds.

DAILY WIN Your game stops looking like a template and starts looking like YOUR world.

W8 Demo Day 1

🕹️ Warm-up

The 60-Second Pitch game: explain your game to a partner in one minute. They repeat it back. Did it survive?

💡 Big idea

Shipping is a skill: polish, playtest, publish, present. Done beats perfect.

✏️ Design corner

First impressions: title screen, instructions, and the first 10 seconds decide if anyone keeps playing.

🔨 Build sprint

Final polish pass + publish to the gallery + safety checklist sign-off.

🛡️ Trust check

Cross-playtest: every kid tests another kid's game and files one kind, useful bug report.

DAILY WIN Families play the games. Builders take questions like pros.

MODULE 2

The Studio

W9 The Talking Buddy

🕹️ Warm-up

Personality Mixer: roll a character (a pirate librarian? a shy robot chef?) and write its three rules.

💡 Big idea

System prompts: an AI character is personality + job + boundaries, written down. You can design a mind's manners.

✏️ Design corner

Character design: voice, catchphrases, what it will and won't talk about. Guardrails are part of the character.

🔨 Build sprint

Build a chat buddy with a job: a homework cheer coach, a riddle master, a tour guide for your game world. Teacher-supervised AI access.

🛡️ Trust check

Red-team your buddy: try to make it break its own rules. Patch the prompt.

DAILY WIN A character you invented holds a real conversation in character.

W10 Give It a Voice

🕹️ Warm-up

Sound Off: the same game clip with three soundtracks — how does each change the feeling?

💡 Big idea

AI does audio too: text-to-speech, sound effects, music. Sound is half of "feel."

✏️ Design corner

Sound design: pick a voice for your buddy, a victory sound, a wrong-answer sound. Loud ≠ good.

🔨 Build sprint

Give your talking buddy a voice and add a full sound pass to your quest game.

🛡️ Trust check

Voice cloning talk: why we never clone a real person's voice without permission.

DAILY WIN Your character speaks out loud for the first time. (Goosebumps.)

W11 Lights, Camera, AI

🕹️ Warm-up

Shot Caller: storyboard a 4-panel scene from a one-line plot. Compare boards — same plot, wildly different films.

💡 Big idea

Directors plan before AI generates. A storyboard is a prompt sequence — the same skill from the book project, now in motion.

✏️ Design corner

Pacing and shots: wide shot, close-up, reaction. Three shots tell a story.

🔨 Build sprint

Make a 20–30 second trailer for your game or buddy: storyboard → AI frames → assemble with titles and your Week 10 sound.

🛡️ Trust check

Deepfake talk: real vs. generated video, and how to spot it.

DAILY WIN A real trailer for a thing you actually made.

W12 Find a Problem

🕹️ Warm-up

Problem or Complaint? — sort real kid quotes: which ones could a product actually fix?

💡 Big idea

Entrepreneurs are problem hunters. Week 4's empathy skill goes pro: interview 3 people (family counts) about a real annoyance.

✏️ Design corner

The problem card: WHO has the problem, WHEN it bites, WHY it matters. No solutions allowed yet — that's the hard part.

🔨 Build sprint

Sketch 3 possible solutions on paper. Pitch to a partner. Pick one with their feedback.

🛡️ Trust check

Would AI help here, or is it AI-for-the-sake-of-AI? Honest answers only.

DAILY WIN A real problem, found by you, with a solution direction you chose.

W13 Brand Studio

🕹️ Warm-up

Logo Lightning: famous logos with names removed — why do you still know them instantly?

💡 Big idea

A brand is a promise people can recognize: name + look + voice, repeated everywhere.

✏️ Design corner

Name your venture, AI-generate 10 logo directions, pick and refine ONE using your Week 7 style-guide skill.

🔨 Build sprint

Build a one-page site for your venture: what it is, who it's for, one screenshot, one button.

🛡️ Trust check

Original check: does your name or logo copy someone? Search and prove it's yours.

DAILY WIN Your venture has a name, a logo, and a live page.

W14 Launch It

🕹️ Warm-up

Price Is Right, Kid Edition: guess what real things cost to make vs. what they sell for. Meet the word "margin."

💡 Big idea

Three honest ways to launch: sell it (business), give it away (open project), or serve a cause (nonprofit). All three are real wins — each kid picks.

✏️ Design corner

The offer: what exactly does someone get, and what do you ask for — money, a donation, or just feedback?

🔨 Build sprint

Finish the venture page with your model. Money handling stays parent-managed; charity ventures pick a real cause.

🛡️ Trust check

Promises check: does the page only promise what the product truly does?

DAILY WIN Your venture is launchable — with a model YOU chose on purpose.

W15 Going Live

🕹️ Warm-up

Crash Test: what could go wrong on launch day? Funniest realistic disaster wins.

💡 Big idea

"Live" means real users and shared data: a class leaderboard everyone's game writes to. With real users come real responsibilities.

✏️ Design corner

Leaderboard ethics: nicknames only, no shaming, what happens to last place?

🔨 Build sprint

Wire your game into the shared class leaderboard and run a live tournament.

🛡️ Trust check

What data does the leaderboard store? Who can see it? Delete a record together.

DAILY WIN Your name climbing a LIVE leaderboard everyone built together.

W16 Pitch Night

🕹️ Warm-up

The Hook game: write the first sentence of your pitch. Class votes on which one makes them lean in.

💡 Big idea

Founders tell the whole story: the problem, the human, the build, the brand, the launch — 3 minutes, live audience.

✏️ Design corner

Slide design speed-run: big idea per slide, readable from the back row.

🔨 Build sprint

Rehearse with the trailer (W11), the live demo, and the venture page (W13–14).

🛡️ Trust check

Final gallery safety review — every project, every rule, signed off.

DAILY WIN A real pitch, a real audience, a certificate, and a portfolio that's all true.
