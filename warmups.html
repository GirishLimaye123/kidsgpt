Design Detective

Two designs walk in. One is better. Tap it and find out why — these are real rules that real designers get paid to know.

Case 1 — the start screen

✅ A. Rule: one clear next step. Every extra choice makes the brain work harder. Start screens have one job — start.

Case 2 — readable or not?

✅ B. Rule: contrast beats pretty. If players squint, they quit. Designers test this with a real tool called a contrast checker.

Case 3 — the error message

✅ A. Rule: errors should help, not scare. Say what went wrong and what to do next, in human words.

Prompt Telephone (solo round)

AI does exactly what you SAY — not what you MEAN. Pick the prompt that gets what the builder actually wanted.

The builder wants: a scary-but-fun ghost for a kids' game

✅ B. Specific beats vague: looks, mood, colors, and a "do NOT" list. In class we call this giving AI a recipe, not a wish.

The builder wants: AI to fix ONE bug without wrecking the game

✅ A. Describe the bug, when it happens, and say "change nothing else." Pros call surprise breakage a regression — you'll hunt some in Week 3.
